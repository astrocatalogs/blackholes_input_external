Description of the Black Hole Mass Calculations<br>
<br>
<table width=75%>
 <tr>
  <td><font color="white"><p align=left>
  Black hole masses are calculated as: <br><center>
  M<sub>BH</sub> = &lt; f &gt; R V<sup>2</sup> / G <br></center><br>
  R is taken to be <it>c &tau;<sub>cent</sub></it>, the speed of light times the emission-line 
  time delay measured from the centroid of the cross-correlation function around the peak,
  which is a measure of the light-crossing time between the broad emission line region and 
  the (much smaller) accretion disk.  
  V is taken to be  &sigma;<sub>line,rms</sub>, the second moment of the emission line 
  profile in the variable part of the spectrum, which is a measure of
  the line-of-sight velocity of the gas responding to the continuum 
  variations.  G is the gravitational constant.<br><br>

  The scale factor &lt; f &gt; is the population-average multiplicative scaling factor 
  required to bring the M-&sigma;<sub>*</sub> relationship for AGNs into agreement with 
  the relationship for quiescent galaxies. Details can be found in the references
  provided for each determination of <nobr>&lt; f &gt;</nobr> above.   The uncertainty 
  on &lt; f &gt; is not included in the determination of the final mass uncertainty. <br><br>

  The final black hole masses provided here are the weighted averages of the individual 
  masses determined from all the emission lines from all reverberation experiments 
  for a specific object.  The user is also presented with the black hole mass based only 
  on measurements of H&beta; on the detailed page for each object.  Masses based on 
  other emission lines or combinations of emission lines may be calculated by the user
  from the reverberation measurements included on the detailed page for each object.  
  Please note that other combinations of reverberation measurements may require the adoption 
  of different values of &lt; f &gt;<br><br>

  Please refer to the <a href="http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2015PASP..127...67B">PASP article</a> 
  describing the database for more information.<br><br>
